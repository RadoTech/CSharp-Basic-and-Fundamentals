2. Maiden's party:
Michaela wants to organize and pay for her maiden's party on her own. She plans to make the payment with the proceeds of her online store. Write a program that calculates the profits from her sales.
Prices of different items:
• Love Message - $ 0.60
• Wax rose - 7.2 BGN
• Key holder - 3.60 BGN
• Cartoon - BGN 18.20
• Luck Surprise - 22 BGN
If the items ordered are 25 or more, the store discounts 35% of the total price. Of the money earned, Michaela should also provide a 10% hosting cost. To calculate if the money will go to her to pay the girl's party.
Input:
The console reads 6 lines:
a. The price of the maiden's party - a real number in the interval [1.00… 10000.00]
b. Number of love messages - integer in the range [0… 1000]
c. Number of wax roses - integer in the range [0… 1000]
d. Number of key chains - integer in the range [0… 1000]
e. Number of cartoons - integer in the range [0… 1000]
f. Number of lucky surprises - integer in the range [0… 1000]
Output:
The console prints:
• If enough money is printed:
o "Yes! {leftover money} lv left."
• If the money is NOT sufficient, the following is printed:
o "Not enough money! {needed money} lv needed."
The result must be formatted to the second character after the decimal point.
Example input and output:
Input:
40.8
20
25
30
50
10
Output:
Yes! 795.75 lv left.
